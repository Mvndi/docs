⚗ The Brewer's Manual
FERMENTING · DISTILLING · AGING · BARRELS
I
Fermenting ALWAYS REQUIRED
  1. Place a Cauldron over fire or a heat source
  2. Fill it with water
  3. Right-click to add ingredients
  4. Wait for the recipe's cooking time
  5. Fill brew into glass bottles
💡 Right-click the cauldron with a Clock to check current fermentation time.
II
Distilling SPIRITS ONLY
  1. Put fermented bottles into a Brewing Stand
  2. Add Glowstone Dust as filter in the top slot
  3. The filter is never consumed — leave it in permanently
  4. Each run = 1 distill pass. Spirits need 1 or 2 runs
  5. Over- or under-distilling degrades quality
⚠️ Each distill pass is one full Brewing Stand cycle. Count carefully — precision matters.
III
Aging ALWAYS REQUIRED
  1. Build or place a barrel (see types below)
  2. Open and place your bottles inside
  3. Every Minecraft day (20 min) = 1 year of aging
  4. Some recipes need a specific wood type
  5. Wrong wood = reduced quality on those recipes
🪵 Recipes that say "Any" work in any barrel. Recipes that list a specific wood (Acacia, Spruce, Birch) REQUIRE that barrel type.
BARREL TYPES

IV
Quality & Sealing OPTIONAL

Quality is hurt by wrong cook time, wrong distill runs, or wrong barrel wood.


To sell via shop plugins, craft a Brew Sealing Table (2 bottles over 4 planks) and seal each batch. Sealed brews can no longer age.
