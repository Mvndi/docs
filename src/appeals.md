# Appeals Process

---

## 1. What is an Appeal?

An appeal is a formal request to have a punishment reviewed. If you believe a punishment was applied incorrectly, unfairly, or disproportionately, you have the right to appeal it. Appeals are not a guarantee of reversal — they are a review process.

Appeals are taken seriously. Submitting a genuine, well-reasoned appeal will always be read by a staff member. Submitting an appeal just to waste staff time, or submitting the same appeal repeatedly after a decision, will result in your appeal privilege being suspended.

---

### 1.1 — What Can Be Appealed

- Temporary bans of any length
- Permanent bans
- Permanent mutes
- Discord server bans
- Point record disputes — if you believe points were logged incorrectly

---

### 1.2 — What Cannot Be Appealed

The following punishments are **permanent and non-appealable** under any circumstance:

- Doxxing — leaking private personal information about a real person
- Any joke referencing pedophilia or child abuse (Rule 2.6)
- Chargeback / payment reversal (Rule 10.0)
- Permanent bans issued to alt accounts used for ban evasion

> If you are unsure whether your punishment is appealable, submit an appeal anyway and staff will inform you.

---

## 2. Before You Appeal

Before submitting an appeal, go through this checklist. Appeals that ignore these steps are usually denied faster than ones that address them.

**Ask yourself:**

- Do you know exactly which rule you were punished for? If not, find out first.
- Do you understand why the staff applied that punishment based on your point record?
- Do you have a genuine reason the punishment was wrong — not just that you disagree with the rule?
- Has enough time passed? Appeals submitted within 24 hours of a punishment are rarely productive.

> ⚠ Saying *"I didn't know the rule"* is not grounds for a successful appeal. Rules are publicly available. It may result in a point reduction at best, not a full reversal.
> ⚠ Saying *"it wasn't that serious"* is not grounds for a successful appeal. Severity is determined by the point system, not by your personal assessment.

---

### 2.1 — Valid Grounds for an Appeal

- The punishment was applied to the wrong player
- The evidence used was incorrect, fabricated, or taken out of context
- The rule cited does not actually cover what you did
- The point total used was calculated incorrectly — e.g. expired points were counted
- A staff member with a conflict of interest handled your case
- New evidence has emerged that was not available at the time of the ruling
- The punishment tier applied was clearly disproportionate to the violation

---

### 2.2 — Not Valid Grounds

- You disagree with the rule itself
- Other players have done the same thing and weren't punished (report them instead)
- You didn't mean to break the rule
- You are friends with a staff member and expect them to overturn it
- The punishment has already expired and you just want it off your record

---

## 3. How to Submit an Appeal

All appeals are submitted through the **designated appeals ticket channel** in the Mvndicraft Discord server. Do not DM staff members directly about appeals — they will be ignored.

---

### 3.1 — Submission Format

Your appeal must include all of the following. Incomplete appeals will be put on hold until the missing information is provided.

| Field | What to Write |
|-------|--------------|
| Your In-Game Username | The username your account was punished under |
| Your UUID | Found at namemc.com — paste your username to find it |
| Date of Punishment | Approximate date the punishment was issued |
| Punishment Type | e.g. 7-day ban, permanent ban, permanent mute, Discord ban |
| Rule Cited | Which rule number or section was cited by staff |
| Your Case | A clear, factual explanation of why you believe the punishment was wrong. No insults, no emotional language. Facts only. |
| Evidence (if any) | Screenshots, video, or other material supporting your case. Not required but strongly recommended. |

> Keep your appeal factual and calm. Aggressive, accusatory, or disrespectful appeals are immediately dismissed regardless of merit.

---

### 3.2 — One Appeal Per Punishment

You may only submit **one appeal per punishment**. If your appeal is denied, you may not re-submit the same appeal.

If significant new evidence emerges after a denial, you may submit a new appeal specifically citing that new evidence. Repeating the same arguments is not a new appeal.

Submitting duplicate appeals, spamming tickets, or harassing staff over a denied appeal will result in your appeal privilege being suspended.

---

## 4. What Happens After You Submit

### 4.1 — Timeline

| Stage | Target Timeframe |
|-------|-----------------|
| Acknowledgement — CM confirms receipt and logs the appeal | Within 24 hours |
| Initial review — CM checks the case file and point record | Within 48 hours |
| Decision for warnings and mutes | Within 72 hours of submission |
| Decision for temporary bans | Within 5 days of submission |
| Decision for permanent bans | Within 7 days of submission |
| Communication of decision to player | Within 24 hours of decision being made |

> These are targets, not guarantees. Complex cases may take longer. If your appeal has gone past these windows with no update, you may send one follow-up message in the ticket.

---

### 4.2 — Who Reviews Your Appeal

- **Warnings and mutes** — reviewed by the Community Manager alone.
- **Temporary bans** — reviewed by the Community Manager and a Moderator jointly.
- **Permanent bans** — reviewed by an Admin. The CM prepares the case file; Admin makes the final decision.

The staff member who issued your punishment does not participate in reviewing your appeal. Any staff member with a conflict of interest (faction, personal relationship, rivalry) is excluded from the review.

---

### 4.3 — Possible Outcomes

| Outcome | What it Means |
|---------|--------------|
| Upheld | The punishment stands as issued. Your point record is unchanged. |
| Reduced | The punishment is reduced — e.g. a 7-day ban becomes a 3-day ban, or points are adjusted downward. |
| Overturned | The punishment is removed entirely. Points are cleared from your record. |
| Dismissed | Your appeal was submitted in bad faith, was a duplicate, or was otherwise invalid. No review was conducted. |

> If your appeal is overturned and the punishment was the result of staff error, the staff member responsible will be noted in the Staff Records.

---

## 5. After a Decision

### 5.1 — Accepted / Reduced

- Any active ban or mute will be lifted or adjusted immediately.
- Your point record will be updated to reflect the corrected total.
- You will be notified in your appeal ticket.

---

### 5.2 — Denied

- The punishment remains in full effect.
- You will receive a brief explanation of why the appeal was denied.
- You may not re-appeal the same punishment unless significant new evidence exists.
- If you believe the appeal process itself was conducted improperly, you may raise a process complaint — see Section 6.

---

### 5.3 — After a Permanent Ban Appeal

If a permanent ban appeal is denied, the ban remains permanent. There is no further appeal path.

If a permanent ban appeal is upheld, you will be unbanned. Your point total will be **reset to 110** — the 30-day ban threshold — rather than zero, reflecting that the violations that led to the ban still occurred.

> A successful permanent ban appeal is not a clean slate. It is a second chance. Any further violations will be treated accordingly.

---

## 6. Process Complaints

If you believe your appeal was not reviewed fairly — not because you disagree with the outcome, but because the **process itself was improper** — you may raise a process complaint.

**Valid reasons for a process complaint:**

- A staff member with a known conflict of interest participated in your appeal review
- Your appeal sat past the stated timeline with no communication
- The decision given had no explanation attached
- Evidence you submitted was not acknowledged in the decision

**Not valid reasons:**

- You disagree with the decision
- You feel the reviewing staff member dislikes you
- You want another chance after a denial

> ⚠ Process complaints are reviewed by a different Admin than the one who handled your appeal. Frivolous process complaints will result in your appeal privilege being suspended.
